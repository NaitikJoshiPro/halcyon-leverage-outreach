## 2. Product / Service Lines

### Study-abroad counselling (core)
The original and largest line. Places **10,000+ students annually**, up from ~1,500 a few years ago.

### Nursing pathway / "Become a Registered Nurse"
Run primarily under the **Work Global** division and the **Leverage Careers** sub-brand. Three nursing/caregiver tracks:
- **RN — USA**: NCLEX-RN + state licensure; advertised salary $5,300–$6,000/month; pathway to direct employment + permanent residency.
- **RN — Germany**: B1/B2 German required; €2,800–€3,500/month; Anerkennung recognition process.
- **Caregiver — Japan**: N4/N3 Japanese; $3,500–$4,000/month.

Program structure is four-phase: **Prepare → Land → Settle → Grow**.

> **Verification gap:** Public sources do not state how many nurses Leverage has placed in the US specifically, nor name any US hospital, clinic, or agency partner.

### Leverage Careers (talent placement)
Dedicated B2B/B2C platform for global hiring. Verticals: **healthcare, education, hospitality**. Works with verified employers in Germany, Japan, USA, etc.

**Major recent move:** Late 2025 / early 2026, Leverage Careers signed an **MoU with the Tamil Nadu Skill Development Corporation (TNSDC)** under the state's *Vetri Nichyam* scheme to train and globally place **120+ nursing candidates per year** starting in Vellore. This is their first formal public-private nursing pipeline.

### Sub-brands
- **Fly Finance** — education-loan marketplace.
- **Fly Homes** — student accommodation. ₹100 Cr GMV in <6 months in UK pilot; now in Türkiye and other markets.
- **UniConnect** — virtual education fair connecting students with global universities.
- **Leverage Live** — live test-prep classes.
- **Leverage MBBS** — medical-school admissions abroad.
- **Compass** — visa/consular services.
- **UniValleyOS** — university-side recruitment SaaS.
- **Learn Online** — online degrees.

### Existing Affiliate Partner Program
Open to anyone (typically agents/influencers/educators):
- ₹500 per submitted application
- ₹20,000 per converted student
- Customised commission plans for "reputed partners" (negotiable)
- Top partners earn up to ₹10 lakh/month
- "1-1-1 Guarantee": application in 1 day, decision in 1 week, commission paid within 1 month
- Real-time UTM-tracked dashboard

**Strategically important: Leverage is already culturally and operationally comfortable running referral/commission programs with external partners. The rails exist.**

### Network & reach
- **3,000+** mentor/counsellor network
- **200,000+** app/community users
- **155,000+** "lives changed" (cumulative)
- **10,000+** education consultants in B2B partner network
- **7.5M monthly site visitors**

## 3. Recent News & Strategic Direction (2024–2026)

- **Geographic pivot away from US-bound students.** US H-1B and student-visa tightening (incl. proposed $100K H-1B levy) hurt their core India→US corridor. Vietnam visas +180%, Brazil +130%. Leverage actively rerouting demand to Germany, Japan, LATAM, Africa.
- **Healthcare is now an explicit growth vertical** under Leverage Careers — TNSDC nursing MoU is the marquee public proof point.
- **EBITDA-positive** in FY26; revenue ₹375 Cr; IPO prep underway.
- **Airbnb partnership** (2024) to promote short-term student stays.
- **No reporting found** of layoffs, controversies, or partnership announcements with named US healthcare entities.

## 4. Market Position & Audience

- **Core customer:** Indian students aged 17–24 applying to undergrad/grad programs abroad; rapidly broadening to working professionals (especially nurses, caregivers, hospitality) seeking overseas employment, plus students from Nigeria, Nepal, Vietnam, Brazil.
- **Cumulative reach:** 155,000+ students served over 9 years; 7.5M monthly visitors; ~10,000 placements/year.
- **Brand strength:** Top-tier in Indian study-abroad ed-tech alongside ApplyBoard-affiliated agencies, IDP, Yocket, GradRight. ETS (TOEFL) backing gives institutional credibility.
- **Alumni-in-US-healthcare data:** **Not disclosed publicly.** Given India is the largest single source of foreign-trained physicians and one of the largest sources of foreign nurses in the US (Migration Policy Institute), and given Leverage's 155K cumulative student base skewed US-bound from 2017–2024, it is reasonable to assume thousands of alumni are now in US healthcare — but this is inference, not a verified Leverage statistic.

## 5. Why a Halcyon × Leverage Edu Partnership Makes Sense

Five concrete angles, ordered by strength of fit:

**1. Their alumni community is structurally adjacent to Halcyon's buyer.**
Leverage has 155K+ alumni and a 200K+ app community, with a measurable cohort working in US clinics and hospitals as RNs, NPs, residents, partners, and physician-owners. Halcyon's referral commission (25% first month + 10% recurring) is ideally suited to a passive side-income offer to alumni who interact daily with practice managers and physician-owners. Leverage already has a referral payout dashboard built for the study-abroad affiliate program — the rails exist.

**2. Leverage Careers is now actively placing Indian RNs abroad and openly courting US employer relationships.**
The Work Global page explicitly markets a USA RN pathway with "30K+ jobs." Halcyon could be positioned as a **complementary services partner** to Leverage's US employer-side outreach: when Leverage talks to US clinics about hiring a foreign-trained RN, Halcyon can be presented as a billing-services upsell to the same clinic. Two monetisable conversations per employer.

**3. Halcyon as a sponsor / featured partner inside the TNSDC nursing pathway.**
The TNSDC MoU explicitly trains nurses for global placement and starts at 120/year scaling up. Halcyon could co-fund or co-brand the "what life looks like in a US clinic" segment of training and embed itself in alumni newsletters — low-cost top-of-funnel into a community that becomes Halcyon's natural distribution post-placement.

**4. Co-marketing into the Indian-American physician/practice-owner network.**
Indian-American physicians own a disproportionate share of US independent practices, especially primary care, internal medicine, and cardiology. Leverage's brand is trusted by Indian families; co-branded outreach lands at materially higher rates than cold.

**5. Operational fit with Leverage's existing affiliate engine.**
Halcyon's commission economics (25% month-1 + 10% recurring) are richer per-referral than Leverage's current ₹500 / ₹20,000 student-affiliate payouts and produce **annuity revenue** — fits Leverage's IPO narrative of recurring, non-counselling revenue.

> **Honest caveat for the pitch:** Leverage's current strategic momentum is *outbound from India* (placing nurses abroad), not inbound services into US clinics. The pitch must frame Halcyon explicitly as a **monetisation layer on top of relationships Leverage is already building** — alumni + US employers — rather than asking them to build something new.
