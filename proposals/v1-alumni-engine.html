The thesis

Your alumni already work
where our buyers are.

Leverage Edu has placed 155,000+ students across nine years and is now actively training Indian RNs, NPs, and caregivers for the US market. Indian-Americans are the single largest immigrant physician group in the US, and a measurable cohort of your alumni are now embedded inside US independent practices — as nurses, residents, partners, and practice owners.

Halcyon's buyer is the practice owner or practice manager at a 1–10-provider US clinic. Our highest-converting channel is warm introduction — 72% of medical billing contracts in the US are won through referrals. A nurse who has worked in that clinic for two years is the exact warm introduction we'd otherwise pay $300–$800 to generate cold.

The proposal is simple: open a Halcyon track inside the Leverage partner dashboard. Alumni who have landed in the US opt in, send a one-line introduction to their practice owner, and earn ongoing income on top of their salary.

Why it converts
Warm intros from staff close at 8–12×.
A cold call to a US practice manager closes ~2%. A warm intro from someone they trust closes 18–25%. The economics of paying 35% gross commission only make sense when conversion is high — and it is.
Why it's defensible
Alumni hold the relationship, not Halcyon.
A competitor can't poach this channel. The trust lives in the alumnus — and the alumnus's payout depends on the practice staying with Halcyon for the long run.
Why it's IPO-friendly
Recurring, non-counselling revenue.
Halcyon commissions hit Leverage's books monthly, with no marginal cost — the alumnus does the work. Every signed practice compounds the line indefinitely.
